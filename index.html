About

Engineering-trained (Penn MS + BS), I started my career in enterprise security at Microsoft — working on Sentinel across CI/CD tooling, billing infrastructure, and PLG and AI adoption initiatives — then moved to Capital One Shopping to build retail media from scratch. Microsoft Special Stock Award recipient. I've shipped across developer tooling, enterprise SaaS, consumer retail, and AI, which is a weird combination that turns out to be useful.

I think most product work fails not because teams can't build, but because they can't decide what matters. Good PM work is mostly subtractive: killing the feature that sounded good in the meeting, dropping the metric being gamed, finding the one thing that actually moves the needle. Data and AI raise the stakes on that — more capability means worse decisions if measurement isn't rigorous. Clarity over complexity. Outcomes over features.
