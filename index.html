About

Systems Engineering (Penn MS + BS), I started my career in enterprise security at Microsoft — working on Sentinel across CI/CD tooling, billing infrastructure, and PLG and AI adoption — then moved to Capital One Shopping to build retail media from scratch. Two reasons: I wanted a 0→1 problem, and I wanted to work at consumer scale. Microsoft Special Stock Award recipient. I've shipped across developer tooling, enterprise SaaS, consumer retail, and AI, which is a strange combination that turns out to be useful.

I think most product work fails not because teams can't build, but because they can't decide what matters — and can't measure whether they got it right. I instrument before I build and kill features that can't be measured. That's the actual job: find the signal in the noise, then make a decision most people would hedge on. Data and AI raise the stakes — more capability without better measurement is just faster failure. Clarity over complexity. Outcomes over features.
